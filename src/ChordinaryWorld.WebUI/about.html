Who are you?

Ahoj! My name is Peter, I am a developer from Czechia. Music is my main hobby so I always wanted to intersect my interests.

What does this service do?

Chordinary World brings you the number of harmonies in a song.

What do you mean by harmony here?

Kinda philosophical question but I would say it is a composition of sounds that embraces some stable part of the melody.

And how is it different from a chord?

A song can have multiple chords dancing around one harmony.

Here are the chords for the verse of Can't Take My Eyes Off You: 
                     C     
You're just too good to be true
              Cmaj7 
Can't take my eyes off you
               C7
You feel like Heaven to touch
        F
I wanna hold you so much
             Fm
At long last love has arrived
             C
And I thank God I'm alive
                 Dm         Dm7
You're just too good to be true
               C
Can't take my eyes off you
Now, C, Cmaj7 & C7 are different chords, however the latter two are just flavours upon C.
Likewise, Dm7 is a flavour upon Dm.
So, we have 7 different chords but only 4 harmonies.

What does this mean?

Not much. Having even the most basic chord progression, a song can be rich in arrangement, or melody, or rhythm, or lyrics.
Having even one harmony, a song can be great!

Seriously.
Listen carefully to The Beatles - Tomorrow Never Knows.
Another example is George Thorogood - Bad To The Bone.
Or, The Guess Who - American Woman.

What is the point then?

Well, if you think to learn a song, the service can tell you how difficult it will be. Less harmonies means easier to learn, as you can simplify chords to harmonies and still sound good.
Also, all the logic is server-side thus you can use its API if you suddenly need it.

How does it work?

Very generally, Chordinary World currently looks for chords at Ultimate Guitar, extracts the chords themselves and does some theoretical analysis on that.
